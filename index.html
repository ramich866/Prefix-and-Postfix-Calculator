Brief Explanation

Instructions: Separate the operators and operands with space!

Infix notation :

Standard way of writing expressions, with operators placed between operands.

For example, (a+b)*c is an infix expression.

Prefix notation : " - + 51 / 60 3 2 "

Places the operator before its operands.

For instance, *+abc is the prefix notation for the infix expression (a+b)*c .

Postfix notation : " 20 3 + 4 1 - * "

Places the operator after its operands.

For example, ab+c* is the postfix notation for the infix expression (a+b)*c .


These notations are often used in compiler design and calculator implementations

as they simplify expression evaluation and parsing.
